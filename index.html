    Howdy, I am Alexander Crabtree! I was born, raised, and am currently still living in Texas. I am a recent Alumni of Texas A&M University where I graduated with a B.S. in Mathematics and Minors in Physics & Astrophysics. It should be no surprise then that I love these subjects. I am currently working with Texas A&Ms AGGIENOVA research group, the research I have been focused on the most has been writing Python scripts to gather data to further develop the AGGIENOVA Swift Supernovae Database. I have also been working to create Python scripts that take a Supernova Type input and uses Supernovae from the AGGIENOVA database to return either a filter light curve graph, a color light curve graph, or a color-color diagram of all relevant Supernovae. In my free time I challenge myself by learning new things, I enjoy reading immensely, and I have been working on my Shogi skills!

    Graphs

    All graphs are created using supernovae from the Swift's Optical/Ultraviolet Supernova Archive (SOUSA). Below are a few light curve graphs I created for type Ib, Ic, and IIP supernovae using primarily the Plotly Python Library. There is also a link to the site that I am hosting a dash app that dispays a Color-Color Diagram of all supernovae types in SOUSA. These graphs are best viewed on a computer, to select a specific supernovae or supernovae group in the graphs below double click on the name in the legend.


    Swift Supernovae UVOT Filter Light Curve Graphs

    • This graph dispays a light curve for the UVOT filters of Type Ib Supernovae (SNe) from SOUSA. The graph starts with all filters light curves dispayed together. You can choose to look at all SNe of a certain filter by selecting the filter from the Filter drop-down menu. To look at the error measurements of the filters absolute magnitudes you can toggle the Error Bar button. Finally, you can toggle specific SNe by double-clicking SNe in the Legend, to view multiple click once on a SNe you want to toggle after the initial double-click.
